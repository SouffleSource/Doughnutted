About Doughnutted

Welcome to Doughnutted.com, the sweet spot where cybersecurity meets confectionery!

In our office, we have a delicious tradition that serves as a tasty reminder of the importance of computer security. We call it "doughnutting," and here's how it works:

• If an employee leaves their computer unlocked and unattended, they're at risk of being "doughnutted."

• Any observant colleague can use the unattended computer to visit this website (doughnutted.com).

• Once doughnutted, the forgetful employee must buy a box of doughnuts for the entire office.

This practice isn't just about satisfying our sweet tooth. It's a practical demonstration of a crucial cybersecurity principle: if someone has enough time to pull up a picture of doughnuts on your unattended computer, they also have enough time to do something far more malicious.

Doughnutted.com serves as both a fun prop for this tradition and a logging system to keep track of offenses and doughnut debts. It's all in good spirit, promoting better security habits while fostering a culture of awareness (and occasional indulgence).

Remember, locking your computer when you step away isn't just good practice—it's the key to avoiding a doughy debt!
